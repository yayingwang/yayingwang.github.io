Furnisher

Making bargain-priced used furniture transaction for nervous incoming & busy graduating students as easy as buying and selling to a friend.

  • Project Type

    Interactive Design Course Project

  • Time Frame

    Oct. - Dec.2018

  • Role & Responsibility

    Individual Project

  • Design Methods Used

    Wireframing,
    Storyboard,
    Prototyping,
    Personas,
    Sketching and Brainstorming,
    Competitive Analysis

Problem Statement

For a lot of students, buying and selling used furniture is a pressing task when moving in and out of school. However, the existing platforms make the transaction process overburdened in looking for items, communicating for item inquiry and pricing. In addition, buyers and sellers are also limited in their options due to the lack of transportation.

Solution Overview

The target solution, Furnisher, is an application that provides complete functionalities to support the whole transaction process of used furniture end to end, from finding suitable items, communicating back and forth, negotiating the price to arranging delivery or pick up.

user research

Competitive Analysis

I analyzed 10 existing platforms, including direct, indirect and partial competitors, for their services in buying and selling second-hand products, specifically furniture. Studying and differentiating their strengths, weaknesses, and brand differentiators gave me a solid understanding of what is already been done and what is being overlooked and can be improved.

Interviews

I interviewed 6 buyers and 2 sellers to understand the pain points in their experience.

  • Buyers and sellers are both under certain time pressure.
  • Overburdened communication for item inquiry, pricing negotiation, and delivery arrangements.
  • Limited transportation restrict the buyers to only small/nearby items.
  • Trust / Reliability issue when doing transactions online.

Personas

After doing all the interviews, I created several personas to identify the target users of this solution. I created one primary persona as buyer and seller to prioritize target user I would like to serve. In addition, I also created one anti-persona to remind myself who are not I am designing for.

These personas delineated the goals they have in building their living environment, frustrations in the existing solutions, and their technology usage to tailor their experience.

Design

Brainstorming & Sketching

In the first step of approaching solutions, I ideated and sketched out several alternatives, including existing and new ones. This helped me expending my imagination and further evaluate how different solutions solve the problem, which can be later used in finalizing my solution.

Design Decision and Defense

In designing for the key feature of Furnisher, the communication between the buyer and the seller, I explored three design alternatives with design rationales.

  • One-to-All message broad.
  • One-on-one chat room + Enhanced functionalities
  • FAQ section in item page

QOC

Then, I used the QOC (Question-Option-Criteria) analysis to evaluate the pros and cons, as well as the trade-offs between the three options. This helped me to think logically what should be a better option in terms of different aspects of consideration.

Paper Prototypes

In order to quickly validate my design ideas, I created paper prototypes for the first two alternatives to test the workflow and the clarity of the information.

Digital Prototypes I & II

Next, I incorporated some of the feedback to revise the design and created the mid-fidelity interactive prototype to further test my design for another two rounds. These user testing helped me realized some major points that I had overlooked or the users might be confused about.

For the group message board, I found that people had mixed opinions about how the display of information and the logic of the workflow.

  • One participant thinks the answers from the seller should be on top of the questions other buyers wrote because those are all he cares about, while other people found that to be confusing.
  • Some people are confused by the mechanism of asking questions.
  • Most of the people prefer to have a separate page for the action of offering bids.
  • Most people are confused about the number of buyers in the broad with the number of bidders.

Design Changes

Based on testing feedback, I made several design changes.

Message Broad - Offering Bids
  • I redesigned the offering bids action to be in a separate page with more instructions.

"Offering bids is important to me. It's my money! I wanna know what's gonna happen before doing that."

Previous Version of asking questions.
New Version of asking questions.
  • In the previous version, the users have to choose to add a question to the existing question thread or to ask a new question, which is confusing to them.

"Why do I ask a question and then there is a button to ask another question? That makes me feel weird"

  • I replaced this step with simply choosing the question type when writing down their questions, and then the system would decide whether to post a new thread or not.
Message Broad - More information.
  • I hide the information of the number of buyers after I found out that not only people would be confused by that but they also do not care very much about that information.

"I don't actually care how many people are in this board. Because some of them may be not seriously considering buying, right?"

Final Design

KEY FEATURE 1
Message Board + Auction
  • All interested buyers can gather and ask questions to the seller in one place. Question threads are organized by different question types.
  • After a buyer decide to buy the item, he/she can offer a bid within the price range set by the seller within the auction duration.

KEY FEATURE 2
Furnisher Bus

A delivery solution for buyers/sellers who lack transportation.

  • After a deal is made, the buyers and sellers can set up delivery buses by selecting the option the system provides based on their address or filing their own Furnisher Bus request and wait for enough people to sign up.
  • The shipping fee would be evenly distributed on all the sellers and the buyers. On the delivery, the bus will first pick-up the item from the seller. When it is delivered to the buyer, he/she has to show the proof of successful payment in order to take the item away.

KEY FEATURE 3
Buyer's Wish List

Not only sellers can decide what to sell, buyers can also post wish lists to attract interested sellers. This function would replace the usual way of transaction with a more effective two-way interaction.

Social Impact

  • By increasing the social interaction between incoming students and graduating students during transaction, Furnisher would hopefully foster relationships or experience shared by the two parties.
  • Prolong the product life of furniture/goods/electronics by raising the likelihood of selling the item instead of simply throwing it away.
